Project 3

The final figma project was another redesign of an existing creation, the US Bank website . However, this one was in tandem with my peers, meaning that while redesigning, we had to maintinan a sense of creative cohesion. With that we shared several assets and decisions along the way to make the production of this project easier and result in a better redesign.
My focus was redesigning the US Bank Mobile App Customer Support section , and the original had some key problems:

  • All help regarding multiple subject was on the page at once. This was disorganized and a lackluster implementation that forced users to have to sift through all help to find specifically what they need.
  • The help was in drop down menus, leading to two consequences: While it helped mitigate the fact that all material was on the same page, it did not help people who may attempt to use a common keyboard shortcut such as Control+F to find the actual content they need; in addition, it could result in an accessiblity issue regarding screen readers.
  • There was no overview of what was on the page nor a table of contents, making the reader responsible for having to find out what is on the page themselves.



My US Banks Mobile App Customer Support redesign is a considerable overhaul. I aimed to accomplish several keys goals:

  • Add an overview for readers to identify if they are in the correct place and a Table of Contents that can jump a reader to the content they are most interested in.
  • Expand the text and the support into an introductory guide rather than miscellaneous problems. This allows entirely new users to gain a firm foundation of the app and its abilities. This would help with keeping features from being underutilized as well.
  • Make the text immediately readable without any complications. This benefits both viewers who are quickly attempting to find their content, less experienced users who may hesitate investigate the drop downs, and screen readers that attempt to read the text on the screen.



The header was slightly redesigned, most of it being copied over. I worked to recreate the header buttons and search bar in Figma, but you will likely notice it is not too different from the original. The primary change made is in the "Breadcrumbs" part.
I am implying that the Guides, Troubleshooting, and more are more broken up on a previous page, this page being just one of many various guides or other types of informative blogs on the website. This organizes the work far better than putting all potential problems across all subjects regarding mobile banking on a single page.



I noticed that a critical part that the original Mobile App Support missed was an Overview and a table of contents. This made it the readers to be able to know if they have ended up in the correct location. If the reader followed a link and did get to this point via following the US Bank website structure, it would likely be confusing without an overview. The table of contents allows a reader to click and jump directly to the content they need on the page, rather than going section-by-section searching for what they were actually looking for.



For the final primary part of my redesign, I created, from nearly nothing, a guide on how to use the US Bank mobile app. This is because the original makes little effort to on-board users that are not already experienced with mobile devices and apps. This guide goes through all the features and abilities of the app that a new user would be most interested in. Each section has a step-by-step guide that carefully leads new users through the process of using the features that might otherwise be neglected.
The guides start with an overview that conveys what this section is about. For a reader, they can determine themselves if this content is important for them as the follow move through each section. Skipping a section -- other than initial setup wherein app installation is explained -- does not make any future section more difficult as they were written with jumping around in mind.
In addition, the guides are not hidden within drop down menus, making them easy to scan through and find what was being looked for. Screen readers will be capble of reading these easily now, and Control+F searches now can immediately find content a user is looking for or tell them it is not hear without the hassle of opening the menus.
